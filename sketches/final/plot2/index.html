PLOT 2: How my social and creatives days evolve through time

Hypothesis:

As the days I gathered as data increase, the time I spend with friends also increases. Days where I spend more time drawing also seem to match the days that feel more socially active.

This was chose because:

I think it was important to use the date as one of the axes. There is a lot to learn when you can actually see the passing of time. This plot connects each day with a coloured area, letting me see whether my social time is rising, falling, or staying consistent. The top area adds the third value used, the area shows how much time I spend drawing that day. I can notice which days were not only socially full, but also creatively engaging.

Technical Implementation:

For this graph I mainly used .map(), d3.stack(), and the d3.area() generator. With .map() I converted each day in my dataset into a simple index number, which made it easier to place everything correctly along the X-axis. Then I used d3.stack() to put my two values (drawing time and time with friends) on top of each other. Stacking was new for me, but now I understand that it basically adds the values together so the chart can draw the bottom layer first and then the layer on top. After that, the d3.area() tool let me actually draw the colored shapes. The two functions, y0 and y1, define the bottom and top of each area, so that’s what creates the “mountain” look.
