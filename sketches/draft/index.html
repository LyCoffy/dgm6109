My Data

This collection of data tracks some of the activities I’ve been doing more often lately, mostly because I made new friendships where we encourage each other to stay creative just for fun.

I collect the date, the time I spend talking to my friends, the time I spend drawing, the number of pictures I take, and my emotional states in the morning and at night.

This dataset helps me analyze how social activity and creativity may relate to my emotional state or vice versa depending on how I choose to visualize it.

Sketch 1 — Time spent with friends / Evening Emotion

Hypothesis: As time spent with friends increases, my evening emotional state improves. Days with more drawing time also tend to have calmer evenings.

Variables used:

  • timeWithFriends
  • emotionalState.night
  • drawingTime

Units:

  • Minutes with friends (0–317)
  • Evening emotional state level (0–5)
  • Minutes drawing (0–120 - each number is change by 0.5 pt. so it wont be too big)

Representation:
A scatterplot where the Y-axis shows time spent with friends and the X-axis shows evening emotional state. Each point is drawn as a circle, and the size of the bubble changes based on drawing time that day.

Sketch 2 — Time spent with friends / Photos taken

Hypothesis: As the days progress, the higher the time I spent with friends. Days with more pictures seem to highlight more socially active days.

Variables used:

  • date
  • timeWithFriends
  • picturesTaken

Units:

  • Minutes with friends (0 - 500)
  • Number of pictures taken (1–30)
  • Dates (Oct 23 – Nov)

Representation:
A line graph where the X-axis shows each day in order and the Y-axis shows minutes spent with friends. At each point where the day and minutes meet, a circle is placed that grows or shrinks depending on the amount of pictures taken.

Sketch 3 — “Christmas Lights” Date / Emotional state

Hypothesis: As I spend more time with friends, both my morning and night emotional states tend to brighten. Higher social activity leads to more consistent emotional levels throughout the day.

Variables used:

  • date
  • emotionalState.morning
  • emotionalState.night
  • timeWithFriends

Units:

  • Dates (Oct/Nov/December)
  • Emotional state (0–10 scale)
  • Minutes with friends (0–500)

Representation:
A horizontal “string of lights” timeline where each light represents a day. Morning emotion appears as a bulb above the wire, and night emotion appears as a bulb below. Color shows emotional level, and the glow intensity of each bulb increases based on the time spent with friends.
